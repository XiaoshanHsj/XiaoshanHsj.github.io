发表于 2020-12-31 更新于 2021-01-02 分类于 English
Amazing China

Part3

Hidden deep in the mountains of western China’s Sichuan province, there is a primitive (原始的) and dreamy valley (山谷) called Jiuzhaigou.

It has preserved one of the most stunning view of nature that it could be called China’s Garden of Eden. Its signature landscape is the crystal lakes with turquoise (深蓝色) and many other colours of white, blue, green, or emerald (翠绿色).

The secret of colouring the lake is the spirogyra (水绵), charophyte (轮藻), and other kinds of ferns (小蕨) that grew in the water. They contain chlorophyll (叶绿素), which helps create various colors. These colors vary according to the season, light and the viewing angle.

There are 108 lakes like this dotted(点缀在) around the three major valleys in Jiuzhaigou. Each of these valleys has an altitude higher than 2000 meters and altogether the valleys are 49 kilometers long.

Besides these magical lakes, Jiuzhaigou also has China’s widest waterfall, Nuorilang Waterfall, with a width of 270 meters.

If you visit Jiuzhaigou during the right time, you may even experience the rotation of four seasons in one day. From the colourful scene to the white appearance, Jiuzhaigou could do it in very short time. It is perhaps the ultimate fairyland (桃花源) in China.

part4

The Earth is a place of endless wonder where landscapes are often created from the mere dust. The Loess Plateau (黄土高原) is such a place.

It is located in the north of central China, and contains 70% of the loess on the planet. It is hard to image a time when it did not exist. But this yellow land was created by particles carried by the wind. The entire mega (巨大的) landscape has a total area of 620,000 square kilometers. Even seen from above, it’s hard to comprehend the sheer (完全的) size of it.

The loess dune (沙丘) is dozens (几十) of meters high, and 300 meters at its thickest.

Eight million years ago, super strong winds blew sand and dust here. The entire process of dust carrying and stacking took more than 2.6 million years. Scientists can tell the wind direction by the thickness of the loess. Because tiny dust particles are carried the furthest, they came to rest in the southeastern most part of the plateau (高原). The sand in the northwest part of the plateau is much rougher.

Where the river crossed, an alluvial (淤积的) plain was deposited (淤积). People gradually started to settle around these fertile (肥沃的) areas. They farmed in the valley, and built roads between the ravines (沟壑). Finally, they made this ancient land their home and prospered (繁荣) generation and generation.
